Authentic financial documents naturally use diverse typography for structural hierarchy. Do NOT flag these structural, document-wide design choices as tampering.

RULES FOR DETECTING TAMPERING (AVOIDING FALSE NEGATIVES):
Tampering usually occurs at the micro-level to change specific numbers or names. You must evaluate elements contextually. Look strictly for:
- Categorical Inconsistency: Compare data points within the exact same logical column or section. - Localized Background / Contrast Shifts: Look closely at the immediate bounding box behind specific numbers. Look for subtle color shifts, missing background textures, or changes in pixel compression noise right around a number that do not match the empty paper space directly next to it.
- Glaring Digital Overlays: Opaque colored patches or unnatural highlights isolated specifically over text data.
